I was born in Seoul in 1988 and have studied and worked in Montreal and Tokyo.

Can speak in French, English, and Japanese, I developed a habit of observing cultures closely to better understand their nuances and sensibilities. These experiences strengthened my adaptability, cultural awareness, and learning ability.

I majored in English Literature and South Slavic Languages at university in Korea, later studying CAD and French in Montreal. I then worked for over two years at the Zara Head Office, directly handling U.S. and Canadian clients. During this time, I realized how web design and motion could enhance the value of products, which sparked my passion for web design.

In 2024, I completed a Japanese language course in Tokyo and studied Web design in a specialized program at Digital Hollywood STUDIO.
With my customer service experience, I’ve learned to always see from the client’s perspective. By combining this mindset with my knowledge of web design, my goal is to become a web designer who contributes to solving both client and user challenges. Furthermore, I aspire to blend the refined sensibility of Korea, the delicate beauty of Japan, and the practical design of Canada in my creative expression.

As a side note, I enjoy cooking and exploring good food. I love iced café lattes, but I can give up caffeine entirely when I’m on a diet.

STRENGTHS

  • 01

    Proactive Problem-Solving Mindset

    When I had trouble getting my visa in Canada, I stayed calm and tried to understand the situation clearly. I found out that a local member of parliament used to work for the immigration office, so I contacted him and explained my goals and how I could contribute to the community.
    With his help, I was able to get my visa successfully. Through this experience, I learned how to identify problems, think of effective solutions, and take action with confidence. In web and UX/UI design, I use the same attitude. I observe, analyze, and test to understand users’ needs and solve their problems through design. Being active and taking initiative, instead of just reacting to issues, has become an important part of how I work as a designer.

  • 02

    Empathetic Communication

    While working at ZARA Canada Head Office in the U.S. administration department, I communicated with customers from diverse age groups and cultural backgrounds. I always tried to understand their situations and identify what they truly needed, as well as the challenges that needed to be solved. Through this experience, I developed strong listening skills and learned how to clearly understand others’ emotions and intentions. In web and UX/UI design, I believe the same approach applies when working with clients. I listen carefully to discover their hidden needs and reflect them in design and user experience solutions.

  • 03

    Cultural Awareness & User Understanding

    By speaking French, English, and Japanese, I have been able to closely observe cultural nuances and understand how people’s values and aesthetics differ. These experiences have made me more adaptable, open-minded, and sensitive to design that connects across cultures. While working at ZARA Canada Head Office, I discovered how design and motion can enhance the way people perceive products, which inspired my passion for IT and web design. My goal is to create beautiful and practical designs that address challenges and bring satisfaction to both clients and users.
